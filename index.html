§ 02 · OverviewAt a glance
What it is

A two-app system for the family economy.

Rewardify is a chore-based reward platform that pairs a playful kid app with a calm parent app. Coins are the unit of trust between the two — earned for completed tasks, spent on parent-approved rewards, and grown in a metaphorical "garden" that teaches the basics of saving and investing.

§ 03 · Why it mattersThe problem
The problem

Money is invisible to kids — and chores are a fight.

Cash disappeared. Cards mean nothing to a six-year-old. Kids don't see effort → reward, and parents end up nagging or paying without context.

  • No effort loop — pocket money is detached from the work it's meant to reward.
  • No visible saving — money in a bank account is an abstraction, not a story.
  • Parent friction — tracking who did what, who gets paid, and what they bought is a mess.
  • No financial literacy on-ramp — kids hit 18 with zero intuition for compounding, risk, or trade-offs.

§ 04 · SolutionThree pillars
Solution

Earn it. Spend it. Grow it.

Rewardify wraps three loops — a chore loop, a shop loop, and a garden loop — into one app the kid wants to open every day, while keeping the parent in light-touch control.

01

Earn — Missions & quests

Parents set tasks with coin values. Kids tap to complete; some auto-approve, some go to parents for photo verification. Streaks, badges and weekly quests keep it fun.

02

Spend — Family shop

A parent-curated catalog (LEGO, books, gift cards, screen time, family treats). Kid checks out in coins; parents fulfill with real money or experience.

03

Grow — Coin Garden

Coins planted in a 3×3 garden grow week-by-week through 7 plant types — each modeling a real financial product, from savings to crypto.

The garden is the emotional hook. It turns abstract compounding into a thing kids visit, water with attention, and harvest with pride.

§ 08 · Kid appLearn
Kid · Learn tab

Bite-sized money lessons that pay coins.

Each lesson is a 60-second card-deck story (What is interest? · Wants vs needs · Why we save). Finishing a lesson awards XP-coins so learning is its own paid mission.

  • Tied to garden — unlocking new plant types requires beating the relevant lesson (Acorn → "What is a deposit?", Sunflower → "How do funds work?").
  • Parent-defined difficulty — pick by age band; lessons get longer and richer as kids level up.

§ 13 · Coin GardenPlant + grow
The growth loop

Pick → plant → tend → harvest.

Tap an empty plot, choose a plant, set how many coins to plant. The cross-section view shows the canopy above and root system below, so kids see real growth — not just a number ticking up.

  • Locked until ripe — sturdy plants can't be dug up early. The bonus is the reward for waiting.
  • Dig-up early — short-cycle plants allow withdrawal at planted-value (no bonus).
  • Harvest celebration — confetti, the option to reinvest or keep coins.
  • Weekly weather — sun, rain, storm, drought. Each plant has its own response curve.

§ 14 · Coin GardenWeather events
Weather as risk model

A weekly weather event teaches risk in human language.

Every Sunday a new weather card lands. ☀️ Sunny weeks lift growth fund-style; ⛈️ storms slam high-risk plants; 🏜️ droughts favor the cactus. Kids see in days what real markets do over years.

  • 4 weather modes — sun, rain, storm, drought — with per-plant multipliers.
  • Public + visible — the chip is permanently in view at the top of the garden.
  • History reel — past weeks render as little stickers, building a felt-record of "what the market was like."
  • Parent-tunable — volatility and weather frequency are settings parents can dial up or down by age.
"My cactus was the only thing that didn't lose coins this week" is the most useful financial sentence a 9-year-old can say.

Once the family economy is in here, switching costs are real. Tasks, history, garden, exchange rate, kid identities — all live.
